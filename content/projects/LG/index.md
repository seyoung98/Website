### Project Overview
**LG Industry-Academia Project:** Self-supervised Learning for Navigation Error and Obstacle Detection in Robot Vacuum Cleaners

* **Project Duration:** Apr 2023 – Jul 2023
* **Collaborating Institution:** LG Electronics

---

### Problem Definition
In complex home environments, autonomous robot vacuum cleaners often encounter navigation failures or struggle to recognize "trap zones"—areas where they consistently get stuck or fail to bypass obstacles due to lighting or surface textures. This project aimed to enhance the reliability of these agents through advanced computer vision.

### Research Approach
I implemented **Image Retrieval** techniques combined with **Model Lightweighting** strategies to ensure real-time performance on edge devices. By utilizing a **Teacher-Student Distillation** framework, we enabled the robot to predict navigation risks autonomously without human-labeled data.



---

### My Key Contributions
* **Technical Literature Synthesis:** Conducted an extensive survey on state-of-the-art Image Retrieval and Self-supervised Learning (SSL) methodologies.
* **Algorithm Design:** Developed a robust detection method applying statistical techniques such as **SVM** and **PCA** to classify navigation environments.
* **Data Pipeline Engineering:** Led the collection and preprocessing of raw navigation data, building a high-quality dataset specifically tailored for domestic obstacle detection.
* **Field Research & Prototyping:** Directly managed robot vacuum units in diverse physical settings to collect real-world "failure data," ensuring the model's practical viability.

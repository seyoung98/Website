abstract: "This study systematically reviewed the literature on women’s colleges in the United States from 1873 to 2025, leveraging BERTopic for an extensive and comprehensive computational review. Originally emerging in the United States and expanding globally, women’s colleges have long served as refuges and communities for women’s higher education and political action—yet, they now face existential questions as women’s status has progressed further than the past, as evidenced by their decline from 230 institutions in the 1960s to fewer than 30 today (Women’s College Coalition, 2014). Women’s colleges have generated a vast body of scholarship to date—and their relevance remains enduring, particularly regarding gender discourse following the introduction of transgender-inclusive admission policies in 2014. Given this landscape, we examined the following research question through a systematic literature review methodology (Newman & Gough, 2020): how have themes and priorities in U.S. women’s college research shifted and evolved across distinct historical periods? We included scholarship from any year available via electronic searching, a decision that led to a robust initial corpus of 21,769 records. Subsequently, we systematically screened them per predefined criteria and conducted backward and forward citation tracking to yield a final corpus for the BERTopic analysis. Then, we divided the dataset into seven periods marked by historically relevant events (e.g., the 19th Amendment, Title IX of the Education Amendments, the turn of the 21st Century). Preliminary findings from our ongoing analysis are [1] the identification of research dimensions: D1 (Institutional), D2 (Student and Alumni), D3 (Specific Populations), D4 (Curriculum and Pedagogy), and D5 (History); and [2] across eras, a decline of “what” is taught (i.e., D4) and an explosion of “who” is served (i.e., D3). In light of this paradigm shift, we discussed insights into topics associated with women’s colleges over time to argue for their importance and raise critical questions."

### My Role & Contributions
As a co-author and data analyst, I designed and implemented the **complete BERTopic analysis pipeline** to process a large-scale corpus of 21,769 records.

* **Data Pipeline Engineering**: Implemented a robust computational pipeline to handle the initial corpus of 21,769 records, ensuring efficient data cleaning and preprocessing for topic modeling.
* **Advanced Topic Modeling**: Leveraged **BERTopic** to conduct a systematic review, identifying five core research dimensions: D1 (Institutional), D2 (Student and Alumni), D3 (Specific Populations), D4 (Curriculum and Pedagogy), and D5 (History).
* **Temporal Analysis**: Divided the 152-year dataset (1873–2025) into seven distinct historical periods (e.g., 19th Amendment, Title IX) to track the evolution of research priorities.
* **Insight Generation**: Discovered and visualized a significant paradigm shift, highlighting a decline in curriculum-focused research (D4) and an explosion in research regarding specific populations (D3) served by women's colleges.
* **High-Impact Publication Preparation**: Currently designing the visualization and analytical framework for a manuscript targeting the *Review of Educational Research* (top-tier journal in the field).
